## The Logic Error Here

The 30,000 EGP saving **goes to the client, not away from you.**

Think of it this way:

| | WordPress Dev | You (Laravel) |
|---|---|---|
| Client pays for build | 65,000 EGP | 35,000 EGP |
| Client pays for licenses/yr | 27,200 EGP | 0 EGP |
| Client pays hosting/yr | 6,750 EGP | 3,750 EGP |
| **Client Year 1 total** | **~98,950 EGP** | **~38,750 EGP** |

The client saves ~60,000 EGP in Year 1 by choosing you over a WordPress agency. **That saving is already built into your lower quote of 35,000 EGP.**

## The Right Way to Think About Your Price

Your price is based on:

1. **Your time** — 220 hours of skilled work
2. **Your responsibility** — you own the deployment, bugs, security, launch
3. **Your value to the client** — she gets a professional booking website
4. **Market rate** — not on how much you saved her in licenses

The license saving is your **sales argument**, not your **pricing basis.**

That makes 35,000 EGP sound like **the deal of the century** — because it genuinely is.
